What is this website?

Dataforme is a safe space built for you.

Here you can learn how to download and interpret the data that you generate online. Hopefully by the time you leave this page you will know how to practice your Right of Access.

My right of access?

You know those messages you send? Your Google searches? Instagram likes, or the music that you stream ...

Most of that is stored in a database somewhere, and it is very likely that someone is profiting heavily from it. But this is your data, and it is my belief that you should have control over it.

Practicing your right of access is as simple as requesting a copy of that information. Since the GDPR went into effect in 2018, you can actually take advantage of that right. Most big web services provide a way for you to get a copy of your digital identity.

Is my data safe here?

Dataforme never obtains a copy of your data. Your data is therefore as safe as you keep it. Be careful where you store your data backup.

You are the one who will have to request a copy of your data, you will also have to download it afterwards.

This website won't automate anything for you. The reason for that is that it leaves you in control.

So I can analyze my data here?

That is correct! The analytics part of this website is designed to work offline. I can guarantee that your data copy is never transferred over the internet.

The beauty of this website however is that you don't need to trust me - just turn off your internet when you attach your file in your browser. By doing so you can rest assured that it is not being uploaded anywhere.

Click on one of the tiles above to get started. You can learn more about your right of access for each web service there.

Full Transparency

This website is Open Source. This means you can view, modify or run a completely local copy of the website if you want. Find the source code here.

This website is still under development. It is my senior project so things will be changing frequently, if this didn't work for you you can let me know here.

Your visit to this website was tracked with Google Analytics, which runs on most of the websites you frequently visit. On my to-do list is figuring out a way to make that data public.

Why does this website exists?

It exists because I downloaded a copy of my information, and got genuinely excited about my Right of Access.

I wrote a few Ruby scripts that provided me with some interesting insights about myself. One of the more obvious things I learned is that I give too much attention to social media.

I think that everyone should be able to gain insights based on their own data, and more importantly should at least know how to get a copy of their information

I had this idea a while back, but I recently made it my senior project!
